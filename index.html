Joe Kutner: Is Sitting the new Smoking?

Author of The Healthy Programmer

To keep doing what you love, you need to maintain your own systems, not just the ones you write code for. Your health affects your ability to learn, remember, concentrate, and be creative–skills critical to doing your job well. But working out at the gym might not be enough to stay fit if you spend the rest of the day sitting in front of a computer. Some surprising new science has revealed that the lifestyle most commonly associated with programming can actually counteract the benefits of exercise. In this talk you'll learn how to change your work habits, boost your brainpower, master exercises that make working at a computer more comfortable, and develop a plan to keep fit, healthy, and sharp for years to come.

Kenneth Reitz: Awareness of Self: Mind Over Matter

Kenneth Reitz is the product owner of Python at Heroku and a fellow at the Python Software Foundation. Kenneth focuses on software development, photography, and music production. He is well known for his many open source software projects, specifically Requests: HTTP for Humans.

Powerful personal experiences are often dismissed by others due to a lack of supporting scientific evidence. Contoversy surrounds the supposed dietary effects of gluten, for example, despite the fact that millions of people claim that removing gluten from their diets has made a profound impact upon their lives. Why is this?

The fact of the matter is that it simply doesn’t matter whether or not gluten has the mechanical effect that is claimed — all that matters is your own direct experience. The placebo effect is very real and proves just how powerful our own perceptions effect our body, mind, and overall wellness.

Kenneth will be sharing his story of self-transformation of body and mind over the past two years. Following your heart.

Katelin Holloway: The Necessary Blank Space

SVP of People and Culture, Whil

Katelin’s first computer was an Apple IIe. She was 3 years old. She’s spent the last 30 years on quest to unplug. Partner at whil Concepts, a health and wellness platform geared to bring mindfulness to the modern person, Katelin is passionate about enabling people to live happier, healthier, and more engaged lives. Her journey includes a 5 year stint at Pixar Animation Studios, building some serious #PeopleGeek chops at Klout, a layover at advertising giant McCann Worldgroup, and surviving a particularly wily bunch of first graders as an elementary school teacher. In her quest, she’s learned that it’s OK to hit the reset button and reboot. Sometimes you have to power down to power up and power forward.

In a world where the speed of digital information is exponentially increasing, people need a set of tools to cope with technostress overload before burning out. When you’re always on, the intersection where your personal life and work life meet becomes more complex. Mindfulness is about making the necessary blank space to find your focus, increase your performance, get creative, and enrich your relationships. With technology and training deeply rooted in science, the digital lifestyle that once maxed out our load capacity is now providing an outlet for us to harness the power to unplug.

Douglas Gayeton: Lexicon of Sustainability

Founder of Lexicon of Sustainability

Douglas Gayeton is an information architect, filmmaker, photographer and writer. He has created award-winning work at the boundaries of traditional and converging media since the early 90’s. He is the director of the KNOW YOUR FOOD series for PBS, and author of both SLOW: Life in a Tuscan Town, and the recently released Local: The New Face of Food & Farming in America.

Naming things is hard, not just in computer science. Douglas Gayeton and his project Lexicon of Sustainability aim to create a shared vocabulary that helps us talk about food. We make smarter choices about the food we eat and feed our families when we know where it comes from, and how it's farmed. It all starts with words...
